Obadiah (variant file)

Unicode hypertext edition: November, 2005, by Christopher McCartney

Adjustments from "beta" code edition to this version, 28 March 2006 (RAK):

  • resolved italics from ,,, to html ...
  • resolved latin to italics coding ... to html ...
  • resolved superscripts from 86\\mg (etc.) to 86mg
  • rubricated chapter/verse indicators
  • still unresolved: use of apostrophe type strokes (choose from ' ` ’ ‘ ´ ) among witnesses. Ziegler Introduction notes just two such strokes, what I call the "prime" mark, and to indicate its curliness, the "comma." Obadiah has all the combinations possible of these two marks, prime alone, comma alone, prime comma together, and comma prime together. There is also, apparently, a back tick (under the tilde key) for one MS, which may be a misprint. The file here, though, has many representations of these combinations, including extraneous use of the back tick.
  • resolved: ... for Syriac [Michigan Claremont (Beta) code, LtoR, Syriac font RtoL, Hebrew font RtoL, א ב ג ] or other semitic language fonts [check how various browsers handle this]
  • resolved: ^  [used ╬ ] for hexaplaric asterisk, [use ÷ ?] for obleus (not in Obadiah?)
  • resolved: ^^ [used ↓] for "see second apparatus"
  • resolved: § for section marker
  • resolved: use of . not : or , to separate chapter.verse indicators (originally subscripted)
  • not included: second apparatus (Aquila, Symmachus, Theodotion, etc.)

Further modification, to represent verses as, e. g. 1, or 19, etc. rather than as y1, y19, etc.
File is initially classed as Stage 2. (The Stage 2 designation represents the following elements: unicode Greek, rubrication of verse designation, conversion of x1y1 verse designation to 1.1, superscripting and subscripting where indicated.)
